INTRO
The Spark
Buying a used car from a dealership or selling your current car to a stranger can be a real pain. During my graduate year at USC, a lot of my international friends came to me asking for help on the process of qualifying for their driving license, choosing a car to purchase, test driving it, insurance shopping, and car maintenances. My friends and I decided to establish our own startup company to offer these services in a more formal and official way.
Start the Journey
Unlike most other car tech startups who were doing online car dealership business, our goal was to build an open platform providing transparent information, auto inspection service, warranty, and consultation on car insurance premised on the customer satisfaction and service delivery.

Our mission was to help people in listing their used cars and purchasing used cars with quality, ease and openness. As long as the buyer/seller make an appointment with Tripalink, an Auto Genius will help the user to finish the entire selling/buying process. We decided to develop a website aimed at helping our customers to understand who we are, what we do, how we can help, as well as promote our services.
Buy Safer, Sell Simpler
In order to help users have delightful experiences when buying or selling a used car, I designed a mobile-friendly website with the function of checking the car’s full information, making an appointment and ability to pay online. Either seller or buyer can complete the buying/selling process from the comfort of their own home.

Two parties are involved in this platform; the buyer and the seller. According to their different needs and behaviors, I designed two different flows which ensure that buyer and seller can navigate themselves easily from the landing page.
02
DISCOVERY & ANALYSIS
80% of our workload was concentrated on the buyer’s side, so I decided to focus on this part and ease the process of browsing, test driving and purchasing. The first step was to conduct interviews with the buyers and understand what they want and how they behave. It allowed me to understand their needs, the buying process and identify the pain points they encounter in the process.

Three Over Complicated and Tedious Journeys
“I need to collect enough information before I go”
Buyers tend to do at least three things before they make the decision:
Compare with market price (compare with Cars.com, Edmunds, Truecar.com)
Check history report (using Carfax, Autocheck, etc.)
Perform vehicle inspection (have a friend to test drive, or bring the car to a service station)
Combine
Multiple Tools
So many factors made the process overcomplicated and time-consuming. We mapped out all the tools used to determine how we can combine them together. I wanted to simplify the search process and underline the features the website should have.
Design Insights
Price and manufacturer reputation are important, but nothing is as important as the vehicle itself.
Buyers like to be fully prepared with as much research as possible before walking in to the dealership.
A majority of buyers value the importance of both price and time throughout the shopping process.
Buyers crave openness and transparency. The vehicles should be fully marketed with custom photos, detailed options and colors, and descriptions.
Take the pain of negotiation out of the equation with upfront pricing and focus on the experience of the purchase itself.
Mobile internet has become a force in the auto sales industry.

LAUNCH
After three-month of hard work with one front-end engineer, two back-end engineers, one product designer (myself) on days and nights, we launched our website on Sept 1st, 2015. Here’re some highlights from the success of the launch:
We sold 15 cars in the first two weeks after the launch, while a medium-size car dealer sells 20 cars per month. At that time, we actually had only 36 cars available for sale on the platform.
On peak time, we saw 800+ reviewers browsing our website all at the same time.
In the second month, we got 10+ startup pitch invitations from a top investor/incubator.
We got 5 investment proposals for the angel round from well-known U.S investors and decided to join an incubator located in Los Angeles.
